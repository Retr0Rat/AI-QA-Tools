## Overview
This course provides an in-depth exploration of deep learning, from the mathematics of backpropagation to state-of-the-art architectures. Students gain hands-on experience training CNNs, RNNs, Transformers, and generative models on real datasets, with attention to efficient training, regularization, and GPU acceleration.

## Tools & Technologies
- Python 3.x
- TensorFlow 2.x / Keras
- PyTorch
- CUDA / cuDNN (GPU acceleration)
- Hugging Face Transformers (introductory)
- Weights & Biases (W&B) — experiment tracking
- Google Colab (GPU runtimes)
- TensorBoard
- ONNX (model export)
- NumPy
- Matplotlib / Seaborn

## Topics
- Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs): architecture and backpropagation
- Activation functions and initialization strategies
- Regularization (dropout, batch normalization, L1/L2)
- Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and image classification
- Transfer learning and fine-tuning pre-trained models (ResNet, VGG, EfficientNet)
- Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), LSTMs, and GRUs
- Attention mechanisms and the Transformer architecture
- Autoencoders and variational autoencoders (VAEs)
- Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)
- GPU acceleration and mixed-precision training
- Model optimization: pruning, quantization, distillation

## Projects & Capstone

### Project 1: Image Classifier with CNN
Students design and train a CNN for a multi-class image classification task, experimenting with architecture choices and applying transfer learning from a pre-trained ImageNet model to boost accuracy.

### Project 2: Sequence Model
Build an LSTM or Transformer-based model for a sequence task of the student's choice (e.g., time-series forecasting, text generation, anomaly detection).

### Final Project: Custom Deep Learning Solution
Students independently define a real-world problem, curate or source a dataset, design a deep learning architecture, train and evaluate the model, and present findings in a demo and written report.
